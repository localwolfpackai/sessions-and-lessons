About The human

A sales operator who fell into the command line.

A decade carrying a bag in tech sales, then a hard left into building with AI. This is who's writing — and why I think we've been measuring productivity wrong.

01 — The story How I got here

I started in tech sales at Salesforce. But by 2021 I was less interested in the quota and more interested in the plumbing — knowledge graphs, digital presence, APIs, how machines actually understand a business.

At Yext I went deep on data strategy and SEO. Shoutout to Christian Ward — he taught me more about data than just about anyone, and his writing at bedatable.com is still worth your time. Christian, if you're reading this: thanks for being my Claude before Claude.

That curiosity earned me an early seat doing AI strategy and marketing operations — completely out of sales — at a Fortune 500. I won't name it, but unless you're the rare soul who does their taxes by hand for fun, you've probably used their software.

Then I opened a terminal for the first time. Once I figured out it wasn't the kind you find in an airport, I was off to the races. A couple years later I build with AI every day — client sites, SEO, the systems underneath — and now I help other people and businesses do the same.

I'm not a developer. I orchestrate: I point the tools, hold the standard, and decide what's good enough to ship. Not a 22-year-old influencer, not a lifer pretending none of this is happening — the bridge.

02 — How I work Four operating beliefs
001

Outcomes over output

Productivity was never about volume. The only question that matters: did the right thing actually get better? Counting tasks is how you stay busy and lose.

Right outcomes
002

Protect the space to learn

Room to explore isn't a perk. Skip it and you rack up cognitive debt — and like every debt, the interest comes due at the worst possible time.

Learn → experiment
003

Experiment like it's the job

Because it is. Most of what I try doesn't work — that's not waste, it's how you find the small number of things that do. Balance the bets, keep moving.

Balanced approach
004

Celebrate intelligent failure

A smart bet that missed beats a safe move that taught me nothing. I keep the receipts on both — the wins and the instructive wrecks live on the same shelf.

Intelligent failure
